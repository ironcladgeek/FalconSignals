# Fundamental Metrics Cheatsheet

## Valuation Metrics (What You Pay)

| Metric | Formula | What It Means | Better When | Red Flag |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|----------|
| **P/E Ratio** | Price / Earnings per Share | Years of profit you're paying for | Lower (10-20 typical) | >50 without strong growth |
| **Forward P/E** | Price / Next Year's Est. Earnings | Future valuation expectations | Lower than trailing P/E | Much higher than trailing |
| **PEG Ratio** | P/E / Growth Rate | P/E adjusted for growth | <1 (undervalued) | >2 (overvalued) |
| **P/S Ratio** | Price / Revenue per Share | What you pay per dollar of sales | Lower (1-3 typical) | >10 for mature companies |
| **P/B Ratio** | Price / Book Value | Premium over net assets | Lower (1-3) for value | <1 may signal distress |
| **EV/Revenue** | Enterprise Value / Revenue | Total company value vs sales | Lower | Varies by industry |
| **EV/EBITDA** | Enterprise Value / EBITDA | Valuation vs operating profit | Lower (10-15 typical) | >20 without growth |

## Growth Metrics (Momentum)

| Metric | What It Measures | Better When | Watch For |
|--------|------------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Quarterly Revenue Growth YoY** | Sales growth vs same quarter last year | Higher & consistent | Deceleration |
| **Quarterly Earnings Growth YoY** | Profit growth vs same quarter last year | Higher (20%+ impressive) | Revenue grows but earnings don't |
| **EPS Growth** | Earnings per share increase | Steady 10-15%+ annually | Buybacks inflating EPS |

## Analyst Sentiment

| Metric | Interpretation | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| **Strong Buy/Buy/Hold/Sell** | Wall Street consensus | Heavy "Buy" = high expectations already priced in |
| **Target Price** | Average analyst price forecast | Compare to current price for upside % |

## Risk & Volatility

| Metric | What It Means | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Beta** | Volatility vs market | <1 = less volatile, 1 = market average, >1.5 = high risk |
| **Debt-to-Equity** | Total debt / shareholder equity | <1 = conservative, >2 = risky |

## Quick Rules of Thumb

### Value Investing (Buffett-style)
- ROE consistently >15%
- P/E <20 (or reasonable vs growth)
- Profit margin >10%
- Low debt (<50% debt-to-equity)

### Growth Investing
- Revenue growth >20% YoY
- PEG <1.5
- High ROE (>20%)
- Can tolerate higher P/E if growth justifies it

## Context Matters!

- **Tech/SaaS**: Naturally higher P/S (5-15), lower P/B, high margins (20-40%)
- **Retail/Industrials**: Lower P/S (0.5-2), lower margins (3-10%)
- **Banks/Insurance**: P/B more relevant, lower P/E (8-15)
- **Growth Stage**: May have negative earnings, focus on revenue growth & margins
- **Mature Companies**: Lower P/E (10-20), steady dividends, focus on ROE & cash flow

---

**Remember**: No single metric tells the whole story. Compare companies within the same industry and look at trends over 3-5 years, not just snapshots.
